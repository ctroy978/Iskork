Danks of Iskork

In the chaotic realm of Iskork, the Dank stand out as pint-sized purveyors of pandemonium. These amphibious troublemakers, barely knee-high to a giant, have elevated mischief to an art form. At the core of Dank society lies the sacred "Order of the Bare Bottoms," a prestigious pantsless organization dedicated to the proposition that all trousers are created evil. Their logic, while questionable to outsiders, is firmly rooted in Dank wisdom: one cannot have their shorts pulled over their head if one isn't wearing any to begin with.

The Dank's aversion to pants is matched only by their love for oversized, water-filled boots. This footwear choice serves a dual purpose: protecting their tender amphibian toes from the rough ground and providing a source of soothing water to keep them from drying out. However, their aquatic footwear comes with a notable drawback - every step produces a distinctive "slosh-slosh" sound.

While the Dank are equal opportunity pranksters, they reserve a special brand of chaos for the drama-loving giants. The towering, toga-clad residents of Iskork are irresistible targets for these bare-bottomed menaces. Nothing delights a Dank more than infiltrating a giant's elaborate toga party and replacing the wine with seawater, or unleashing a horde of sea urchins into their marble baths. The resulting theatrical meltdowns and over-the-top lamentations from the giants fuel the Dank's mischievous spirits for days.

Despite their small stature and limited understanding of the world, the Dank have become a force to be reckoned with in Iskork. Their watery antics and bare-bottomed escapades may seem like a joke, but their ability to sow chaos among the other races is a powerful force in the land.

Racial Traits

Danks are surprisingly effective in combat, possibly due to their unpredictable nature.

Their limited understanding of the world often leads them to pay extra when trading with caravans.

The Danks refuse to communicate with the Spenz, maintaining a longstanding feud.
